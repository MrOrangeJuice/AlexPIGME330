Information

John Horton Conway's Game of Life is a cellular automaton, or essentially a simulation taking place in a grid of "cells", created in 1970. The goal of the simulation is to emulate the dvelopment of life in the simplest way possible, by following a series of core rules:

  1. Any live cell with two or three neighbors survives
  2. Any dead cell with three live neighbors becomes a live cell
  3. All other live cells die in the next generation. Similarly, all other dead cells stay dead

The purpose of this website is to give the user access to a much larger world with which to run the simulation, and give them complete control as to how they view it. The simulation world actually extends fairly far beyond the colored border, and more can be seen by clicking and dragging the canvas. I would have loved to have made the world much bigger than it is but unfortunately the site's performance would decrease drastically were it any bigger, and even at this site the performance can be spotty depending on the resolution of your monitor. If you'd rather not scroll, the window itself can be made bigger with the window width and height controls.
